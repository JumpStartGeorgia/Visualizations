Was Your Vote Counted?

Ballots are Missing

For both Proportional and Majoritarian elections in 2012, there are a total of 10,914 ballots unaccounted for.

  • Proportional - 3,051 missing ballots
  • Majoritarian - 7,863 missing ballots

How Did This Happen?

How Voting Works in Georgia

  • The procedures that take place on election day are defined by election code
  • People go to polling station
  • People sign in and get ballots
  • People fill out ballots
  • People turn in ballots
  • Polling Station staff count all ballots and record the totals on a 'protocol'
  • Protocols are sent to the CEC
  • CEC does a double-blind data entry
  • Data is now official

Where Problems Can Occur

  • Election code allows special polling stations to be merged into other polling stations for counting
  • People do not turn in ballot(s)
  • Polling station staff miscount
  • CEC makes data entry mistakes

Election Code and Special Polling Stations

  • A special polling station is a prison, military base, hospital or other facility
  • Votes from special polling stations are merged into a parent polling station
  • In a Majoritarian election, there is no way to properly know how many ballots were submitted from the special polling stations compared to the parent polling station, without a court order opening up the official polling station documents
  • In the 2012 Majoritarian election, there were 74 special polling stations whose voting results cannot be properly verified

People Might Not Turn in Their Ballot

  • Polling Stations count the signatures of people that received ballots, NOT the total ballots put in the box
  • This means if a person signs for a ballot but does not put it in a box, the ballot is missing

Polling Station Miscount

Protocol Explanation

  • 1 - Possible Voters
  • 2 - Special Voters
  • 3 - Votes by 12:00 and by 17:00
  • 4 - Ballots Signed For
  • 5 - Ballots to Hand Out
  • Votes for Each Party
  • 6 - Invalid Ballots Submitted
  • Signatures of Polling Station Staff

Polling Station Miscount

Vote Count Validation

To validate that the ballots were counted correctly, the following equation should be true:


(4 - Ballots Signed For) - (6 - Invalid Ballots Submitted) = SUM(Votes for Each Party)
Here is a protocol from Polling Station 1 in Gori. For this protocol, the equation turns into:
832 - 32 = (4 + 277 + 11 + 1 + 3 + 3 + 6 + 495)
800 = 800
Validation Passed!

Again, how are there more votes than ballots submitted? Did not one of the Polling Station Staff notice that nearly 50% of all ballots signed for were marked as invalid?
In this case, the Polling Station Staff incorrectly filled out the protocol by making the number of invalid ballots equal to the ballots on hand minus the ballots signed for.
(5 - Ballots to Hand Out) - (4 - Ballots Signed For) = (6 - Invalid Ballots Submitted)
650 - 429 = 221
221 = 221

CEC Data Entry Mistakes

Due to the protocol having '0' typed in for Possible Voters, the polling station staff filled in the Possible Voters under the Special Voters section.
When the CEC entered the data, they started with the first hand-written number and did not notice that there was no number for Special Voters, instead entering the Votes by 12:00 number into Special Voters.
Thus all remaining data in the top half of the protocol was entered incorrectly for each of the 46 polling stations in the Overseas district.
Due to this data entry error, the validation equation previously explained would produce an error of 34,097 missing ballots.
To put that into comparison, the district Gori had the largest amount of missing ballots totaling 516.

No
But that does not mean that it will not affect the outcome of future elections.

What Can Be Done to Fix These Mistakes in Future Elections?

Fix Mistakes - Election Code

  • Get rid of special polling stations or require special polling stations to tabulate their own results

Fix Mistakes - Polling Stations

  • Properly train polling station staff on how to fill out the protocol
  • Require Polling Stations to record ballot signatures and the number of ballots submitted

Fix Mistakes - CEC Data Validation

  • Include validation checks when the CEC is entering the data. Validation equation: (Total ballots submitted) - (Invalid allots) = (Sum of party votes)
  • The Polling Station results will be audited if validation errors in the protocol are found
