Was Your Vote Counted?

Ballots are Missing

For both Proportional and Majoritarian elections in 2012, there are a total of 10,914 ballots unaccounted for.

  • Proportional - 3,051 missing ballots
  • Majoritarian - 7,863 missing ballots

How Did This Happen?

How Voting Works in Georgia

  • The procedures that take place on election day are defined by election code
  • People go to polling station
  • People sign in and get ballots
  • People fill out ballots
  • People turn in ballots
  • Polling Station staff count all ballots and record the totals on a 'protocol'
  • Protocols are sent to the CEC
  • CEC does a double-blind data entry
  • Data is now official

Where Problems Can Occur

  • Election code allows special precincts to be merged into other precincts for counting
  • People do not turn in ballot(s)
  • Polling station staff miscount
  • CEC makes data entry mistakes

Election Code and Special Precincts

  • A special precinct is a prison, military base, hospital or other facility
  • Votes from special precincts are merged into a parent polling station
  • In a Majoritarian election, there is no way to properly know how many ballots were submitted from the special precinct compared to the parent polling station, without a court order opening up the official polling station documents
  • In the 2012 Majoritarian election, there were 74 special precincts whose voting results cannot be properly verified

People Might Not Turn in Their Ballot

  • Polling Stations count the signatures of people that received ballots, NOT the total ballots put in the box
  • This means if a person signs for a ballot but does not put it in a box, the ballot is missing

Polling Station Miscount

Protocol Explanation

  • 1 - Possible Voters
  • 2 - Special Voters
  • 3 - Votes by 12:00 and by 17:00
  • 4 - Ballots Signed For
  • 5 - Ballots to Hand Out
  • Votes for Each Party
  • 6 - Invalid Ballots Submitted
  • Signatures of Polling Station Staff

Polling Station Miscount

Vote Count Validation

To validate that the ballots were counted correctly, the following equation should be true:


(4 - Ballots Signed For) - (6 - Invalid Ballots Submitted) = SUM(Votes for Each Party)
Here is a protocol from Polling Station 1 in Gori. For this protocol, the equation turns into:
832 - 32 = (4 + 277 + 11 + 1 + 3 + 3 + 6 + 495)
800 = 800
Validation Passed!

How Many Mistakes Were Made?

Proportional Mistakes

  • 24.37% of all polling stations are missing ballots
  • 75 of 76 districts had at least one polling station that is missing ballots
  • Districts with largest amount of missing ballots in one polling station: Gori - 196; Dmanisi - 87; Gori - 71
  • Districts with highest percent of polling stations with missing ballots: Nadzaladevi - 47%; Saburtalo - 43.82%; Khelvachauri - 43.18%

Majoritarian Mistakes

  • 54.07% of all polling stations are missing ballots
  • All 76 districts had at least one polling station that is missing ballots
  • Districts with largest amount of missing ballots in one polling station: Gori - 208; Bolnisi - 77; Gori - 73
  • Districts with highest percent of polling stations with errors: Telavi - 96.30%; Zestaponi - 94.34%; Zugdidi - 87.16%

What Can Be Done to Fix These Mistakes in Future Elections?

Fix Mistakes - Election Code

  • Get rid of special precincts or require special precincts to tabulate their own results

Fix Mistakes - Polling Stations

  • Properly train polling station staff on how to fill out the protocol
  • Require Polling Stations to record ballot signatures and the number of ballots submitted

Fix Mistakes - CEC Data Validation

  • Include validation checks when the CEC is entering the data. Validation equation: (Total ballots submitted) - (Invalid allots) = (Sum of party votes)
  • The Polling Station results will be audited if validation errors in the protocol are found
